Environmental Voting, Jobs, and Polarization in the US House :

For my senior Statistics & Data Science thesis at Yale, I modeled the changing effect of ideology, partisanship, and constituent material interests (as represented by district fossil fuel jobs) on House environmental voting in an era of increasing polarization (1989-2016). To do so, I estimated the fossil fuel jobs in each member's district during each Congress in the time period, and created a dataset merging fossil fuel job, environmental voting, NOMINATE, and member characteristics data, which I hope could be useful for other analyses.
